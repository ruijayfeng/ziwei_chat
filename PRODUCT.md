# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Ziwei Chat serves ordinary Chinese-language users who are curious about Ziwei
Dou Shu analysis but do not want to learn professional terminology before
asking a practical question. They open the app to understand tendencies, timing,
relationships, work, money, and recurring life patterns through a grounded,
plain-language conversation.

## Product Purpose

Ziwei Chat is an open-source-first vertical agent for Ziwei Dou Shu. It helps
users create one primary chart, ask natural-language questions, and receive
answers grounded in deterministic chart facts, topic skills, curated knowledge,
and response critique. Success means the user can trust what data the product is
using, see why an answer was given, and continue the conversation without login
or SaaS account setup.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, companion-like.

The voice should feel warm and competent. It should not act like a mystical
authority, a cold expert system, or a generic chatbot.

## Anti-references

Ziwei Chat should not look like:

- red-and-gold fortune-telling pages
- parchment, temple, oracle, or antique interfaces
- generic purple AI-gradient SaaS pages
- game-like celestial consoles, starfields, outer glows, or decorative orbit animations
- marketing landing pages that hide the product behind a hero section

## Design Principles

1. Put chat at the center and evidence within reach.
2. Show the chart and data the agent is using before asking for trust.
3. Keep mystery inside order: calm surfaces, precise hierarchy, no theatrics.
4. Use mature UI primitives for standard interactions and spend craft on the
   product-specific experience.
5. Treat every loading, empty, error, and deletion state as part of the product,
   not an afterthought.
6. Use a deep warm-indigo night palette with restrained cinnabar punctuation and
   editorial Chinese typography; motion must explain state rather than simulate mysticism.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for primary product surfaces. Motion should communicate
state, not decoration, and must respect reduced-motion preferences when added.
The UI should remain readable for users who are unfamiliar with Ziwei terms by
using plain Chinese labels and explaining expert vocabulary when it appears.
